Networking Commands for CMD on Windows 11 💻

Hello everyone! Welcome back to the Tech-Savvy Project 🎉 Today we’re diving into the world of networking commands on Windows 11. These are the tools your computer uses to talk to the internet, other devices, and even itself. Let’s explore 12 of them in a way that’s fun, friendly, and beginner-proof. Ready? Let’s go! 🚀

1. NETSTAT

Shows active connections and listening ports. Think of it as your computer’s chat log with other devices.

2. NSLOOKUP

Asks the DNS system: “Where does google.com live?” and gets back an IP address. Like looking up someone’s address in a phone book.

3. PING

Plays Marco Polo with another computer. Sends a signal and waits for a reply to check if the other device is reachable.

4. TRACERT

Shows the path your message takes to reach its destination. Like tracking a letter through every post office it visits.

5. IPCONFIG

Displays your computer’s IP address, subnet mask, and gateway. It’s like your digital home address and how you get outside.

6. HOSTNAME

Reveals your computer’s name tag. Useful for identifying devices on a network.

7. ARP

Matches IP addresses to MAC addresses. Like asking, “Who is 192.168.1.5?” and getting a face to go with the name.

8. GETMAC

Shows your computer’s MAC address—its unique bracelet number on the network.

9. NETSH

A powerful tool to manage network settings. You can reset Wi-Fi, change IPs, and more. Like admin mode in a video game!

10. PATHPING

Combines ping and tracert. Checks each stop along the way and tells you if any are slow or dropping messages.

11. SYSTEMINFO

Displays detailed info about your computer—OS version, memory, processor, and more. Like reading your PC’s resume.

12. TELNET

Connects to another computer and lets you type commands as if you’re sitting at its keyboard. Just be careful—it’s not secure like SSH!

🎉 That’s a Wrap!
