MSG: The way to connect with computers locally! 💬

Hey there, tech explorer! 🚀 Welcome to our very first lesson about computers. Today’s topic? The mysterious and magical MSG command—your secret weapon for chatting with friends on the same Wi-Fi.

You’ve probably used VoIP before (think FaceTime, Skype, Snapchat), but ever wondered what it actually is? It’s short for Voice Over Internet Protocol, and it lets you talk to people using the internet instead of old-school phone lines. Pretty cool, right?

But enough about that—let’s talk MSG. This nifty command lives inside Windows 10 and lets you send messages to other computers on your network using PowerShell or Command Prompt. No extra apps, no drama. Just type and go!

Here’s how you do it: msg * hello world! 🎉

Let’s break it down: msg is the command, the asterisk * means “send to everyone,” and hello world! is your message. You can say anything you want—just keep it friendly (and maybe avoid using your neighbor’s Wi-Fi unless you want a surprise visit 😅).
